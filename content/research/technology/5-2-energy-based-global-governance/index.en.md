## From Gold Standard to Compute Standard

In the 20th century, gold and the USD were the credit foundations of global governance. By the mid-21st century, as AIOS permeates every atom of civilization, **Computing Power** plus **Energy** constitute the new pillars of sovereign credit.

A nation's international status will increasingly be defined by the "cheap, clean computing power" it can stably output.

## Governance Value of Compute Output

1. **Compute-for-Peace Agreements**: Nations with strong compute power can exchange regional stability and resource access by exporting AIOS governance Agents (e.g., agricultural optimization, basic disease diagnosis) to resource-scarce regions.
2. **Energy-Compute Closed-Loop Ecosystem**: Future governance will not lie in controlling land, but in controlling the physical aggregation of energy infrastructure (photovoltaic/nuclear fusion) and hyper-scale computing clusters.
3. **Transborder Intent Scheduling**: AIOS is establishing a global intent network that transcends traditional sovereign borders, requiring governance models to shift from "administrative orders" to "protocol constraints."

## Green Compute Contract

Under this new order, computing power is not to be squandered limitlessly; it must be built upon energy sustainability. Through a "Carbon-Compute Equivalence Protocol" formulated by AIOS, global governance will enter a fairer stage of resource gaming deducted in real-time by physical laws.
